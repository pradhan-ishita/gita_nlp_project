About the Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita is a sacred dialogue between Lord Krishna and the warrior Arjuna, spoken on the battlefield of Kurukshetra in the epic Mahabharata. It presents timeless teachings about duty, righteousness, self-discipline, and spiritual wisdom.

Through its 18 chapters and 700 verses, the Gita explains important philosophical concepts such as Karma Yoga (the path of action), Bhakti Yoga (the path of devotion), and Jnana Yoga (the path of knowledge). These teachings guide individuals toward inner peace, self-realization, and balanced living.

About the Bhagavatam

The Srimad Bhagavatam is one of the most important devotional scriptures in Hindu philosophy. It elaborates the stories and teachings of Lord Krishna and explains the nature of divine love and devotion.

While the Bhagavad Gita focuses on duty and spiritual knowledge, the Bhagavatam highlights devotion and the divine relationship between the soul and God. Together they form the foundation of many spiritual traditions in India.
